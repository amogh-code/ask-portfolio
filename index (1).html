Ventures & Work

Businesses built, products shipped, communities grown.

In 2025, I spent a significant amount of time building Miyog — an AI-driven social media tool that ideates, creates, edits, and publishes content end to end. The product supports both AI-assisted editing of existing videos and fully AI-generated content.

Building Miyog involved fine-tuning LLMs, integrating multiple APIs, writing and managing thousands of lines of code, and owning the system from development to deployment.

What I built
  • Fine-tuned LLMs for content ideation
  • Multi-platform API integrations
  • AI-assisted video editing pipeline
  • Fully AI-generated content flows
  • End-to-end dev to deployment ownership

In late 2025 and early 2026, I built ASK — a personal AI pendant designed for everyday productivity. The device uses an onboard microchip, battery, and microphone to listen to conversations both online and offline, and automatically create notes, set meeting reminders, and generate to-do lists.

Additional capabilities are enabled through API integrations. Building ASK involved hands-on work with hardware design, 3D printing, IoT systems, AI integrations, and end-to-end product ownership.

Technologies involved
  • Custom hardware design & 3D printing
  • IoT systems & onboard microchip
  • AI integrations (notes, reminders, to-dos)
  • Online & offline conversation listening
  • Full product ownership from concept to device

My Philosophy

As someone fascinated by psychology and drawn to existentialist thought, I believe understanding human behaviour is the foundation for building things that actually matter — whether that's a product, a company, or a community.

Psychological Lens

Understanding how people think and behave shapes every product, pitch, and interaction. My psychology background gives me a lens that most builders don't have — I design for humans first.

Stoic Foundation

Drawn to stoicism and existentialism — focus on what you can control, build in the present, take responsibility for outcomes. This keeps me grounded across the chaos of running multiple projects at once.
